Hang the DJ

About the episode


"Hang the DJ" is the fourth episode of Black Mirror. It was written by Charlie Brooker and directed by Tim Van Patten. The episode first aired on Netflix, along with the rest of series four, on 29 December 2017. It follows Amy and Frank, who are matched into relationships for fixed lengths of time by an algorithm that eventually determines their life-long partner.

The episode was inspired by the streaming service Spotify, as Brooker considered a system which gives people "playlists" of relationships.

Plot


Frank and Amy use a circular device called "Coach" that matches them with partners for fixed periods of time. They are matched together for 12 hours. Coach tells them the system monitors each relationship to assign them a lifelong partner on "pairing day", with a success rate of 99.8%.

Frank's next match Nicola immediately dislikes him, but they are paired for a year. Meanwhile, Amy is paired with her nine-month match Lenny. Amy and Frank meet again, at an event where a couple talk about their successful pairing. After the relationship ends, she is repeatedly matched with people for 36 hours.

After Frank's match ends, he and Amy are matched again and they agree not to check the time length, to avoid preconceptions. One night, Frank checks the expiry date. It initially says five years but recalibrates, as Frank's betrayal of their agreement has destabilised it, until it reads 20 hours. Frank is distracted the next day as Amy notices every pebble she skips hits the water four times; he admits what has happened with an hour remaining. She is furious and he is heartbroken.

They continue matches to no avail. The evening before Amy's pairing day, she chooses Frank for her one permitted farewell session. At dinner with Frank, whose pairing day is also tomorrow, she encourages him to leave with her. Recognising that neither of them have memories prior to the system, she thinks the world is a test and they must rebel. Frank and Amy run and scale a wall that separates the outside world. The world is a simulated reality, one of 1000, and one of 998 in which Frank and Amy rebelled. In the real world, Amy's dating application says Frank is a 99.8% match and they make eye contact for the first time across a bar.
